📄 Publications & Patents

Academic Papers
  • C. Jin, J. Hong, H. Sun, et al. "Design and Development of a Positioning System Based on Driven Orthogonal Omnidirectional Wheels," Information Technology, 2028. (Core Journal, First Student Author, Accepted)
    Abstract: Addressing the lack of odometry accuracy in LiDAR SLAM mapping, this paper designs a highly adaptable and precise wheeled odometry device and algorithm. By utilizing high-precision encoders combined with adaptive terrain structures and an integrated IMU state Kalman filter algorithm, it achieves precise positioning of highly mobile devices in various terrains, providing reliable odometry reference for SLAM and 3D mapping with depth cameras.
  • J. Hong, B. Hao, H. He and C. Tang. "Heart Rate Detection System Based on ADS1292 Module," 2025 6th International Conference on Computer Engineering and Intelligent Control (ICCEIC), 2025. ( IEEE Xplore, EI Compendex, First Author, Published)
    Abstract: This paper utilizes the ADS1292 module to extract simulated and real ECG signals, transmits them to embedded devices, and performs frequency domain analysis after signal filtering. This yields key ECG parameters of the subject, including the heart rate index, RR interval, and heartbeat cycle.

🔬 Research & Project Experience

Waterjet Blade Cutting Robot (MIIT "Reveal the List and Take Charge" Project) Core Member | 2025
Joint Enterprise-University Research (Beijing Huaneng Yangtze River Environmental Protection, Hefei General Machinery Research Institute, CQU Robot Application Innovation Lab)

Having mastered SLAM mapping and navigation technologies, I utilized the existing equipment of our lab (Robot Application Innovation Lab - Intelligent Equipment Lab R&D Team, Advisor: Assoc. Prof. Lei Zhang). To overcome the limitations of single-wheel odometry, we adopted a GPS+IMU filtering fusion method for precise positioning of mobile devices. By combining drone-borne LiDAR, we achieved 3D mapping and target navigation in complex, undulating terrains. Based on robotic arm inverse kinematics, I controlled a 6-axis robotic arm to plan the blade cutting route using point cloud data provided by the host computer, constructed a motion trajectory data matrix to avoid singularities, and completed the depth point cloud data interface on the host machine. Furthermore, based on the existing wind turbine blade models, we performed global 3D reconstruction (via depth camera 3D SLAM mapping), outputting a dataset of over 5,000 point clouds. The project is currently integrating large models and deploying Vision-and-Language Navigation (VLN).
